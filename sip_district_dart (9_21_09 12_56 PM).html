District Improvement Plan: 2008-09 School Year
All 15 schools in the Bering Strait are required to do Site School Improvement Plans in DART, and adjust their goals each quarter. Individual BSSD teachers also use the DART SIP Module to create classroom level plans.

The following District Improvement Plan data has been entered by BSSD District Office Program managers and staff, and summarizes the District's efforts for this school year. This is meant to meet the reporting requirements of the Alaska Department of Education and Early Development.

Goals and Action Plans

Emphasis Area :: Reading

District Meaurable Goal


The 3rd-10th Grade students of Bering Strait School District will show improvement in reading skills, as indicated by their performance on the spring SBA & HSGQE standardized tests

Current Performance Level on SBAs


53% or 472 BSSD students were proficient on the reading portion of the spring 2008 state assessments
Performance Target

60% or 543 students to be proficient on the reading portion of the spring 2009 state assessments

Scientifically based research to support each strategy listed below. Can include references or summaries of relevant research:

US Dept of Education sponsored a review of twenty-two comprehensive reform models by the Comprehensive School Reform Quality Center at the American Institutes for Research gave Success for All the highest rating awarded for research quality and outcomes (CSRQ, 2005). Earlier reviews by Borman, Hewes, Overman, & Brown (2003), Herman (1999), Traub (1999), and Schacter (1999) came to similar conclusions. A review of twenty two reading programs by Pearson & Stahl (2002) found Success for All to have the strongest research base of all core reading programs.

Scientifically based research to support each strategy listed below. Can include references or summaries of relevant research:

NWREL research supports and shows that the use of 6+1 Traits in the teaching of writing has increased student achievement. Developed by the Northwest Regional Educational Laboratory (NWREL). This is an analytical writing system developed for teachers and students to instruct and assess important components of skillful writing.

Scientifically based research to support each strategy listed below. Can include references or summaries of relevant research:

Dempster & Ferris; The spacing effect: Research and practice. This foundational research has shown that instruction that presents material over several intervals results in greater student achievement than instruction that is not. Research has also provided evidence that student recall is superior under conditions of distributed instruction than under conditions of massed instruction.
